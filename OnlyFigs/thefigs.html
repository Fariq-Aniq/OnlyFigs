Discover Fig Varieties

Explore the diverse world of figs, each with its unique flavor profile, texture, and origin.

Black Mission

Extremely sweet and often used in desserts. Their skin is deep purple-black with pink flesh.

Brown Turkey

A mild flavor and few seeds. They have brownish-purple skin and light pink flesh.

Kadota

The most common green fig. Lesser sweetness but creamy texture, great for canning.

Nutritional Powerhouse

Why figs are considered a superfood for your daily health.

  • Fiber

Digestive Health

Figs are incredibly high in soluble fiber, which helps maintain a healthy digestive system and promotes gut microbiome diversity.

Blood Pressure Control

Rich in potassium and antioxidants, figs help manage blood pressure and reduce the risk of cardiovascular diseases.

Radiant Skin

High levels of vitamins C and E along with powerful antioxidants help protect skin cells and promote natural glow.

Natural Energy Boost

The natural sugars in figs provide a quick yet sustained energy release, making them the perfect pre-workout snack.

Journey Through History

Figs have been valued for thousands of years across various civilizations.

9400 BC

The First Cultivation

Early records suggest figs were among the first plants to be deliberately bred by humans in the Jordan Valley.

Ancient Egypt

Pharaoh's Favorite

Cleopatra's favorite fruit was said to be figs, and they were often buried in royal tombs as offerings.

Ancient Greece

Olympic Power

Figs were used as highly valued prizes for Olympic athletes and were a staple in their training diets.
